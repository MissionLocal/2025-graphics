Vending issue chronology in the Mission

March 2, 2022 to April 2022

Vending became an issue after the pandemic and by March 2, 2022, Ronen launched a Mission Plan to go after vending, trash, fires and homelessness.

"Street conditions in the Mission are unacceptable," the plan states.

The first plan envisioned permitting vendors so that staffers from the Department of Public works and officers could discern between permitted vendors and those without a permit who were also likely selling stolen merchandise.

This photo was taken on April 25, 2022 — less than two months after Ronen launched the plan.

July 20, 2022 to August 21, 2022

Fences go up. A permitting plan is in the works but has not been launched.

What's the hold-up? Public Works has not finished creating a permit system, meaning none of the consequences for illegal vendors can be enforced, said Ronen.

Until the permitting system is in place, the “nightmare” on the plaza needs to be addressed, as drug dealers and illegal vendors threaten the legitimate merchants and community organizers who are monitoring the area. Fences become the answer.

Late July, 2022

But not the solution.

Even in late July any rider getting off the bus faced a corridor of people in all directions hawking everything from beauty products to roach killer.

Late August, 2022

By late August, the fences came down.

Late August, 2022

And, unpermitted vending continued.

Sunday, August 28, 2022 — The first fatality.

On the afternoon of Sunday, Aug. 28, an argument broke out on the northeast BART plaza between a man and 28-year-old Jabaree Harris over $50, a witness told Mission Local.

September 2022 — Permits have been issued and enforcement begins

Unpermitted vending begins in the morning, but vendors disperse once DPH staffers arrive. Once they begin enforcement, very few vendors remain at the 24th Street Plaza.

September 2022

Sixty-one permits approved and as long as officers are at the plaza most of the unpermitted vendors stay away.

Dec. 18 2022 — Second fatality.

At 4 p.m., a shooting leaves one person dead at the southwest 24th Street BART plaza.

February 2023

The permits are clearly not working. Permitted vendors are sometimes out, but the plazas continue to be overrun by vendors selling goods — likely stolen — out of their backpacks and suitcases.

At a community meeting, the 116 permitted vendors and others are clearly frustrated with the lack of enforcement.

“There’s no hierarchy, we’re all out there to do business and survive,” said Ryen Motzek, president of the Mission Merchants Association.

Susana Rojas, executive director of Calle 24, spoke in support of the permitted vendors. “Those of us from the Mission know we’ve had vendors all along.”

Almost a year after the permit ordinance was passed last March, Rojas believes that we’ve reached the limits of its ability to address activity in the plaza.

June 27, 2023 to September 2023 — Barricades go up again at 24th Street

The sidewalk remains packed, and most of the dozen or so vendors along Mission Street appear to be operating without a permit.

Department of Public Works employees, facing threats and physical violence in nearly a year of a new effort to tackle unpermitted vending, say they want police to take over enforcement.

“We won’t even say anything yet — they’re already threatening our families, threatening to kill us, telling us, ‘Just wait ’til we see you without the police,’ ‘We’ll follow you to your home,’” said one worker named John at the Mission District police station’s latest monthly community meeting.

And at 16th Street

Instead of availing themselves of the entire plaza, vendors now set up their wares in front of the barricades or on the pavement, reducing space for pedestrians.

“It’s not stopping anybody doing anything; it just makes our lives more difficult,” Pinky said.

A police spokesperson said the fencing would address “concerns regarding vendors encroaching on the sidewalk and making it difficult for people to move through the area without obstructions.”

Nov. 13, 2023

After a lot of community meetings, a complete ban goes into effect on Nov. 27 — a temporary ban that Supervisor Ronen is able to get because of the health and safety issues that vending has created.

While there is pushback, others praise the move as a means of dealing with fencers selling stolen goods, and Public Works employees being threatened and attacked. At a Board of Supervisors meeting, several permit inspectors recount the mental strain of being pushed into conflict with vendors, and asked for police intervention.

Nov. 14, 2023

Ronen refuses to abandon the ban and it goes into effect on November 27th with permitted vendors given two physical spots to set up — one indoors on Mission Street and the second at Capp and 24th streets.

Nov. 15, 2023 to present

The bans work at the plazas as long as police and DPW workers are stationed at the plazas. Otherwise, they can quickly become flooded with unpermitted vendors.

December 2023 to 2024

Meanwhile, the Mission Street indoor market and the Capp street sanctioned markets open, but have few customers.

Feb, 4, 2024 — The vending ban is extended for six months

The ban is effective as long as officers and DPW workers are on site.

But as soon as the officers leave, the vendors return.

April to June 2024

The sanctioned spaces for permitted vendors fail to bring in customers.

The indoor market for permitted vendors closes in May. The market at Capp and 24th is also failing.

‘No one comes,’ the vendors say.

May to June 2024

The ban stays in effect, but ten permitted vendors are allowed back on Mission Street.

“We’re happy, because we’re back and there’s more people here, more transit,” said a vendor, in Spanish, of the pilot program on Mission Street between 23rd and 24th streets.

June 19, 2024

Unpermitted vending continues at the plazas and along Mission Street.

August 15, 2024

State Bill to give officers the right to ticket vendors for selling stolen merchandise fails.

San Francisco responds by extending the vending ban for the third time.

Mission District supervisor Hillary Ronen had supported the bill. “We were hoping that we could lift the moratorium once that law passed,” she said. “Now that it hasn’t passed, we’re going to have to keep the moratorium going.”

In October 2024, the vending continues.

February 2025

Scott Wiener reintroduces the bill to allow police officers to ticket San Francisco vendors who are unpermitted.

The vending ban is extended until June 30, 2026.

By early February, it took three police cars to keep the plaza at 24th Street clear.

Present day

The cars leave and the plazas fill up, blocking pedestrians walking on Mission Street near the plazas.
