San Francisco's overall budget for 2024-2025 is $15.9 billion.

Out of that $15.9 billion, $9.1 billion goes to "enterprise departments," in other words departments that generate their own revenue and oversee their own budgets.

Those departments are the SFMTA, the PUC, the airport and the port.

The remaining $6.8 billion goes into the "general fund," essentially a vast pool of money that pays for most day-to-day city operations. That includes all other city departments, and administrative costs.

But out of that general fund, $2.3 billion in funding is earmarked for specific programs. That includes federal and state money that pays for social services like Medicaid, and CalFresh.

Excluding those non-discretionary funds, $4.5 billion remains in the general fund.

But out of that $4.5 billion, another $2.2 billion is baked into the budget, largely because of voter mandates. Over the years, voters have decided to allocate some revenue, often tax revenue, for specific departments and programs.

This is what funds a number of things from library services to street tree maintenance.

That leaves the mayor with $2.3 billion in discretionary funds, or about 15 percent of the overall budget.
